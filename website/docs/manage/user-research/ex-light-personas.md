# Example Lightweight User Personas

This article provides example [lightweight user personas](./user-personas.md#lightweight-user-personas).

You can use these examples as reference material or as a template for writing your own personas. The key attributes to cover when developing personas is their job summary, motivations, pain-points, so that you can support their motivations and design products and services that address or eliminate their pain-points.

## The subject matter expert, but not a data engineer

This persona has deep expertise in their specific STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, and Math) domain, such as biology, physics, or engineering. They are highly knowledgeable in their field but are not specialized in data engineering or advanced data analytics. While working with data is part of their job, it is not their primary focus. They often find themselves doing data preparation and cleaning tasks that distract from their core research or technical work.

1. **Motivations**: Driven by a passion for their subject matter and the desire to make impactful contributions to their field. They are motivated to generate insights and discoveries, even if it means wrestling with data issues that are not their area of expertise.
2. **Challenges**: Faces challenges in efficiently working with data due to limited access to specialized tools, platforms, or expertise. Often has to rely on manual processes, making it difficult to scale or automate their work. Collaborating with other teams and sharing data across institutional boundaries can also be a challenge.
3. **Current Actions**: Engages in data wrangling, manually curating and cleaning datasets for use in their research. Collaborates with other researchers and institutional partners, often sharing data through less efficient means such as email or spreadsheets.
4. **Pain Points**: Frustrated by the time and effort required to prepare and manage data when their focus should be on analysis and research. Lacks easy access to data engineering support or intuitive data platforms that could streamline their work. Data-sharing restrictions and governance issues often add to their workload.
5. **Key Quotes**: "I spend more time cleaning data than I do analyzing it." "I know there are better ways to handle data, but I don't have the time or resources to figure them out."
6. **Opportunities**: Could benefit from tools and platforms that simplify data preparation and collaboration, allowing them to focus more on their core expertise. There's an opportunity to provide them with self-service data solutions that are easy to use and integrate well with their existing workflows, as well as training or support to help them maximize efficiency without becoming data engineers themselves.

## The solution architect starting from scratch

This persona is not a data practitioner and has limited experience in data analytics, though they have a solid understanding of high-level architectural principles. They have a strong grasp of the organization's data-related challenges but lacks hands-on experience in solving them. They often think in terms of big-picture solutions but struggle with the technical details.

1. **Motivations**: Driven by the need to deliver a scalable and effective solution to meet organizational goals. This persona is motivated to succeed in a complex project, even if it means navigating unfamiliar territory.
2. **Challenges**: Faces challenges in translating conceptual goals into practical solutions. Struggles to ask the right questions and has limited access to data experts who can guide them through the nuances of the field.
3. **Current Actions**: Actively researching possible solutions, seeking advice from vendors, and trying to understand the necessary steps to implement a comprehensive data solution. May be assembling a team or seeking partnerships with more experienced data practitioners.
4. **Pain Points**: Feels overwhelmed by the complexity of data solutions and the steep learning curve. Lacks confidence in choosing the right technologies and strategies, leading to delays or indecision. Additionally, struggles with limited capacity and resources to fully engage with the project.
5. **Key Quotes**: "I know what we need to achieve, but I'm not sure how to get there." "There's so much information out there�"how do I know what's relevant to our situation?"
6. **Opportunities**: Could benefit from targeted consulting and guided implementation services to bridge the gap between strategy and execution. There's an opportunity to provide them with clear, structured roadmaps, along with mentorship or access to data experts who can help simplify complex decisions.

## The data practitioner with higher priorities

This persona is a self-taught data engineer or analyst who has learned to navigate the data landscape through hands-on experience rather than formal training. They are primarily focused on completing urgent tasks efficiently and within existing frameworks. Innovation and process improvement are secondary priorities due to a heavy workload and limited capacity.

1. **Motivations**: Driven by the need to meet deadlines and deliver results, often in a high-pressure environment. This persona values practicality and efficiency, preferring proven methods over experimentation with new tools or workflows.
2. **Challenges**: Faces challenges in finding the time and resources to improve their data workflows or adopt new technologies. Struggles with technical debt and legacy systems that hinder long-term improvements.
3. **Current Actions**: Primarily engages in one-off tasks, data wrangling, and problem-solving within the constraints of existing systems. Often sticks to familiar tools and processes to avoid disruptions.
4. **Pain Points**: Overwhelmed by a full workload, leaving little room for strategic thinking or innovation. Finds it difficult to secure the necessary support, resources, or time to make meaningful changes to their workflow.
5. **Key Quotes**: "I know things could be improved, but I just don't have the time or resources to make it happen." "I'm always in firefighting mode, so improving processes isn't a priority."
6. **Opportunities**: Could benefit from solutions that streamline current workflows without requiring significant time investment. There's an opportunity to introduce automation, low-code/no-code tools, or consulting services that help them optimize their processes incrementally without disrupting their current workload.

## The cloud migrator

This persona has a preexisting mature system on-premises and is evaluating how to migrate the system to the cloud. They are an expert in their current system environment and architecture but may have only limited cloud experience.

1. **Motivations**: Driven by the need to modernize their infrastructure, reduce costs, and improve scalability. This persona is motivated to achieve a seamless migration with minimal disruption to existing services.
2. **Challenges**: Faces challenges in translating on-premises architecture to cloud-native designs, particularly in understanding cloud-specific services, cost structures, and security models.
3. **Current Actions**: Actively researching cloud platforms, consulting with cloud vendors, and developing a migration strategy. They are also working to identify which parts of their existing infrastructure need to be rearchitected for the cloud.
4. **Pain Points**: Concerns about potential downtime, data integrity, and security during the migration. Also worried about the learning curve associated with new cloud technologies and the potential for hidden costs.
5. **Key Quotes**: "We know our on-premises environment inside and out, but the cloud is a whole different beast." "How do we ensure that the migration is cost-effective without compromising performance?"
6. **Opportunities**: Needs support with cloud architecture design, migration planning, and cost optimization. Opportunities for cloud consulting services, migration tools, and hands-on training tailored to their specific infrastructure.

## The Excel for everything

This persona relies heavily on Excel for almost all of their data management, analysis, and reporting needs, even when more specialized tools may be available. They have developed a deep familiarity with Excel and uses advanced functions, pivot tables, and macros but may have limited exposure to other data tools or platforms.

1. **Motivations**: Driven by a desire for simplicity and familiarity. Prefers sticking with Excel because it's a tool they understand and trust, and it allows them to work independently without needing support from IT or data teams.
2. **Challenges**: Struggles with Excel's limitations when handling large datasets, performing complex calculations, or collaborating in real-time with others. May also face challenges in maintaining version control and data integrity.
3. **Current Actions**: Uses Excel to manage and analyze data, create reports, and track various projects. Often builds complex spreadsheets to compensate for the lack of specialized tools.
4. **Pain Points**: Frequently runs into performance issues with large files and spends a significant amount of time on manual data entry or cleanup. Concerned about the potential for errors and the difficulty in scaling their work.
5. **Key Quotes**: "Why switch tools when I can do everything I need in Excel?" "I know Excel has its limits, but it's what I'm comfortable with."
6. **Opportunities**: Could benefit from training on more powerful and collaborative data tools that integrate well with Excel. There's an opportunity to introduce lightweight automation or data visualization tools that expand their capabilities without overwhelming them with complexity.

## The buzzword aficionado

This persona is highly engaged with the latest trends, tools, and industry buzzwords, often discussing concepts like AI, blockchain, big data, and digital transformation. While knowledgeable about industry trends, this persona may not have deep practical experience in implementing the technologies they talk about. They are more comfortable with high-level concepts than with hands-on technical details.

1. **Motivations**: Motivated by staying relevant and influential in conversations with peers, leadership, and clients. Enjoys being seen as forward-thinking and in touch with the latest advancements.
2. **Challenges**: Struggles to bridge the gap between buzzwords and actionable solutions. May feel overwhelmed when asked to apply their knowledge practically and finds it challenging to prioritize which trends are most important.
3. **Current Actions**: Frequently attends conferences, reads industry blogs, and shares articles on social media. Actively participates in discussions about emerging technologies, often advocating for their adoption in the organization.
4. **Pain Points**: Faces difficulty in translating broad concepts into practical, scalable solutions. Sometimes their enthusiasm for new technologies leads to confusion or resistance from more pragmatic team members.
5. **Key Quotes**: "We need to adopt AI and machine learning to stay competitive." "Blockchain could revolutionize our industry."
6. **Opportunities**: Needs support in cutting through the noise to focus on technologies that will deliver real value to their organization. There's an opportunity to guide them toward practical implementations and to help them build deeper expertise in key areas.

## The siloed and well-funded data expert

This persona is highly skilled and experienced in data management, analytics, or data science, often working on cutting-edge projects. They have access to significant funding and resources within their organization. They operate largely in isolation from other teams, enjoying a high degree of autonomy and independence in how they use their resources and tackle their projects.

1. **Motivations**: Driven by the pursuit of excellence in their specialized domain. Motivated by the opportunity to push boundaries and innovate without the constraints of limited budgets or organizational red tape.
2. **Challenges**: Faces challenges related to collaboration and knowledge sharing. While well-funded, this persona may struggle to align their work with broader organizational goals or to leverage insights from other teams due to their siloed nature.
3. **Current Actions**: Actively working on data projects with ample resources at their disposal. Often builds bespoke solutions or conducts advanced research without much input from other departments.
4. **Pain Points**: Encounters difficulties in cross-departmental collaboration, leading to duplicated efforts or missed opportunities for synergy. May also feel disconnected from the organization's broader strategy, which can result in a lack of impact or visibility for their work.
5. **Key Quotes**: "I have the resources I need, but I'm not sure if what I'm doing aligns with what the rest of the organization is focused on." "Collaboration isn't easy when you're working in isolation."
6. **Opportunities**: Could benefit from initiatives that promote cross-team collaboration and shared goals, as well as platforms or forums that facilitate knowledge sharing. There's an opportunity to help them connect their work to larger organizational objectives while still allowing them to maintain their autonomy and focus on innovation.
