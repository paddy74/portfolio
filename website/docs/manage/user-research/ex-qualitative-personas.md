# Example Qualitative User Personas

This article provides example [qualitative user personas](./user-personas.md#qualitative-user-personas).

You can use these examples as reference material or as a template for writing your own personas. The key attributes to cover when developing personas is their job summary, motivations, pain-points, so that you can support their motivations and design products and services that address or eliminate their pain-points.

## Stanley, The Empowered Data Program Manager

- **Age**: 43
- **Education Level**: Bachelors in Geography, Masters in Ecology
- **Years at TheCompany**: 10+

### Motivations

- Professional growth
- Pride in working for TheCompany.
- Efficiency and improvement
- Desire to contribute to scientific community.

### Traits

Moved into the current role from within TheCompany.

- Aware of general technology trends
- Limited awareness of modern platform/software development process and gaps in roles
- Relies on specific Vendors and internal semi-technical staff.
- Limited or No consultation with Technology Bodies.

### Current Actions

- Manages improvement programs.
- Crafts technology vision within directorates/divisions
- Acknowledges importance of user testing
- Manages to get platforms and initiatives stood up

### Pain Points

- Last minute security mandates from leadership
- Lack of planning and advise to meet tech obligations.
- Fragmented landscape of software procurement
- Need to knock on a lot of doors.

### Key Quotes

"Lot of times TheCompany puts security and privacy requirements beyond what's written in the guidance."

### Opportunities

- Provide product design and product management expertise.
- Consulting for leveraging cloud services appropriately in data domain.
- A public/community facing "Kaggle" like platform where data, compute, toolchains, and insights are collocated for the end users.
  - An instantiable marketplace offering with custom branding and configurable option for deploying the platform.
  - A hosted and managed instance of this platform to support small programs and individual contributors with limited budget.

## Aisha, Researcher Leading Process Improvement

- **Age**: 37
- **Education Level**: PhD in Mechanical Engineering
- **Years at TheCompany**: 5+

### Motivations

- Passionate about their domain
- Pride in working for TheCompany.
- Want to excel and prove themselves.

### Traits

First job out of school at TheCompany or limited experience outside TheCompany. PhD in a science or engineering discipline but responsible for software engineering, data engineering, and workflow automation.

- Expertise in science and engineering.
- Ability to program and use software tools.
- Limited experience in production grade automation
- Surface level skills in collaborative software development.

### Current Actions

- Leverages various software tools to complete tasks.
- Composes workflows manually using software tools.
- Uses workarounds to address policy constraints.
- Attempts to solve software engineering challenges.

### Pain Points

- TheCompany restriction on tools for partner collaboration
- (Assumed) Limitations of cross language interoperability.
- Lack of an existing tool chain
- High cost and poor support from vendor

### Key Quotes

"Tool chains is one of the biggest things we would like to have, but we don't fully have that capability within TheCompany." "We have to go through different owners and operators of these models to press their various buttons to be able to trigger the analysis workflow." Opportunities

- Software automation & cloud consulting
- Workflow automation platform to stitch different tools together in a pipeline.
- Custom software development

## Micah, Junior Data Scientist

- **Age**: 24
- **Education Level**: Physics Undergrad, Data Science Masters
- **Years at TheCompany**: 2+

### Motivations

- Pride in building high quality products.
- Passionate about cutting edge technologies.

First fulltime job out of school at TheCompany. Prior internship experience in software development.

- Composes workflows manually using software tools.

## Priyanka (Platform Engineer)

### Alternative Job Titles

- DevOps Engineer
- Operations Engineer
- Systems Engineer
- IT Consultant

### Job Summary

My job is to enable the developers to focus on the business code only. To achieve my goal, I write pipeline definitions, deployment templates, CLI tooling to be used by development teams, so the software delivery process is streamlined across organization. Beside building out these features and fixing potential bugs, I educate and support the development teams around topics related to our pipelines or target infrastructures, so they can build, deploy, and release as efficiently as possible.

Depending on the organization and/or need of the development teams, I am part of a central platform team or a member of an application development team.

### Motivations

- When developers plan a project that requires my support, I want to be notified, so I can avoid unnecessary reactive work.
- When I resolve problems, I want to be able to track impact and other important success metrics, so I can raise my profile in the organization.
- When I create a solution for developers, I want to see it being used, so I know that my contributions are reliable and valued.
- When I'm facing a new or novel challenge, I want to whiteboard it with a teammate, so I have the satisfaction of helping solve a problem that has no manual.
- When I'm advocating for a new process or tool, I want to point to a metric or test, so I can support my case with something objective.

### Frustrations

- It is hard for me to quantify my efforts and convince others of time that will be saved in the future by investing time in proactive tasks in the present.
- I'm frustrated by the avoidable crises, caused by a lack of communication, that derail my work and burn up my resources.
- I dislike frequent context-switching and being responsible for some tasks that I feel I am not good at, because of the many hats my job requires me to wear.
- I'm frustrated by the politics of convincing people to adopt my recommendations.
