# Example Technical Debt Policy

This article is an example of the **Technical Debt Policy** section of a [Team Working Agreement](./index.md).

A **Technical Debt Policy** is a framework that governs how technical debt is incurred, tracked, managed, and retired.

## Introduction

**Technical Debt** is the accumulation of sub-optimal implementations requirements over the lifetime of product development. It is caused by the decision to choose an easier solution now instead of selecting a better approach which would take longer. Technical Debt does not directly prevent a product from meeting requirements but does impact the quality of the product and its maintainability. Any software system is vulnerable to the build-up of Technical Debt as the complexity of system increases. As Technical Debt accumulates, its impact becomes more evident in the extra effort required to add new features and address bugs. This impact is considered the interest on Technical Debt.

## Prevention

Proper DevOps, CI/CD, design, and Story Point estimates will reduce Technical Debt. Automating tedious and time-consuming tasks will reduce the time required to develop new Features and therefore the need to select sub-optimal implementations in order to meet delivery targets.

## Resolution

Approximately 10% of work effort for a Sprint is allocated to address Technical Debt. When the Development Team is ahead of schedule for a Sprint and no more User Stories or Bugs exist in the Sprint Backlog, they will prioritize selecting Technical Debt from the Team Backlog.
